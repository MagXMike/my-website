The Battle of Lagos (1759) during the Seven Years’ War

⚔️ Key Facts

- Date: August 18–19, 1759

- Location: Off the coast of Lagos, Portugal

- Belligerents: Great Britain 🇬🇧 vs. France 🇫🇷

- Outcome: Decisive British victory

🧭 Background

- France aimed to unite its Mediterranean and Atlantic fleets to support a planned invasion of Britain.

- Admiral Jean-François de La Clue led the French fleet from Toulon, attempting to slip through the Strait of Gibraltar undetected.

- British Admiral Edward Boscawen, stationed in Gibraltar, was tasked with intercepting them.

🚢 The Battle

- The French fleet was spotted after passing Gibraltar and pursued by the British. - On August 18, the British caught up and engaged the French near Cape St. Vincent. - Fighting continued into August 19 near Lagos, where several French ships sought refuge in neutral Portuguese waters. - The British ignored neutrality and captured or destroyed five French ships.

📉 Consequences

- The French Mediterranean fleet was crippled, leaving only their Atlantic fleet at Brest.

- This paved the way for Britain’s victory at the Battle of Quiberon Bay later that year, securing naval dominance.

- The win helped thwart France’s invasion plans and solidified British control of the seas.
